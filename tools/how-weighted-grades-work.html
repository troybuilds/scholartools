How Weighted Grades Work

Many schools use weighted grading systems to calculate final grades. Instead of every assignment counting equally, different categories such as homework, quizzes, and exams have different weights.

What Is a Weighted Grade?

A weighted grade means that certain assignments affect your final grade more than others. For example, a final exam may count for 30% of your grade, while homework might only count for 20%.

Example of a Weighted Grading System

A typical class grading structure might look like this:

  • Homework: 20%
  • Quizzes: 20%
  • Midterm Exam: 30%
  • Final Exam: 30%

In this system, exams have a larger impact on your overall grade than homework assignments.

How Teachers Calculate Weighted Grades

To calculate your final grade, each category grade is multiplied by its weight and then added together.

For example:

  • Homework average: 90% × 20%
  • Quizzes average: 85% × 20%
  • Midterm: 88% × 30%
  • Final Exam: 92% × 30%

The total of these weighted scores becomes your final course grade.
